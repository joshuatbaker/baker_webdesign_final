Plastic Bottles

Just under a million plastic bottles are sold around the world every minute. The average American buys around 346 plastic drinking bottles a year.

Polyethylene Terephthalate, the plastic in these bottles, is estimated to take over 400 years to decompose. Polyethylene plastic makes up nearly 96 percent of all plastic bottles.

The United States recycles around 9 percent of its plastic waste. Less than a fifth of plastic waste is recyled worldwide.

There were 26 million tons of plastic put into landfills in 2015, according to the EPA. This was 18.9 percent of all municipal solid waste thrown into landfills that year.

>

Plastic Bags

There are around 18 billion pounds of plastic waste flowing through the oceans according to a project done by National Geographic called "Planet or Plastic?"

The same project also said that shoppers in the United States use nearly one plastic grocery bag a day.

>

Cigarette Butts

Cigarettes are small, but when littered leave a large imapct. Keep America Beautiful estimates that only 25 percent of cigarettes are disposed of properly

In addition, 38 percent of cigarette littering occurs in the physical environment, meaning a lot of cigarettte waste is put into nature.

Even smokers themselves often do not have the necessary means to properly dispose of butts.

>

To-Go Cups

To-go cups contain small amounts of uncommon recyclabe plastics, as well as often coming paired with plastic lids and cardboard sleeves.

The Earth Day Network estimates that 16 billion dispoable coffee cups are used each year.

Americans alone throw out 25 billion styrofoam cups yearly. Polystyrene, a common foam used for to-go cups, is not recylable.

>

Cardboard and Shipping Materials

More things are being shipped in cardboard boxes today than ever before thanks to online shopping.

Around 18,280 tons of cardboard were landfilled in the United States in 2015 according to the EPA, however, 45,320 tons were recycled.

Shipping materials often included with cardboard boxes should also be disposed of properly, as many of these materials are not biodegrable or recyclable.
